Incident 2026-04-22: search latency P99 climbed from 80ms to 4500ms over a 6-hour window starting around 02:00 UTC. The on-call engineer didn't notice because the alert threshold was set too high (P99 < 5s) — by the time it fired, customers in EMEA had already filed 11 tickets. Root cause: a gradual fragmentation of our Elasticsearch index due to a missed daily reindex job that has been silently failing for the past 3 weeks. Contributing: our cron alert pipeline was changed two months ago and the failure notifications were going to a deprecated Slack channel. Remediation: tightened the latency alert to P99 < 500ms, fixed the reindex job, audited all cron alerts for deprecated routes, and added a synthetic check that reads from search every 5 minutes.
